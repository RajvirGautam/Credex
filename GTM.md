# GTM.md

## Target user (one sentence)

The **first finance-conscious operator at a 5–50-person AI-native startup** — usually a founding engineer, a head of ops, or a CFO-by-default founder — who notices the SaaS line item creeping but doesn't have time to audit it themselves.

## Pre-trigger search

What they search the day before they'd find us:

- "is cursor business worth it for 2 devs"
- "github copilot vs cursor pricing"
- "how to reduce ai tool spend startup"
- "chatgpt team vs plus for solo"
- "openai api vs chatgpt plus heavy use"
- "claude pro vs team pricing"

These are buying-intent queries, not awareness queries. Each one has a literal recommendation our engine could spit out.

## Where they hang out

1. **Indie Hackers + r/startups + r/SaaS** — long-tail "how do you handle AI spend" threads weekly.
2. **YC Bookface** (we don't have access; partners might).
3. **X/Twitter dev-finance accounts** — a smaller set than dev-engineering Twitter but more receptive to our content.
4. **Operator slack groups** (RevOps, FinOps for startups) — paywalled but a single advocate gets us in.
5. **Producthunt** — week-of launch traffic only; not ongoing.

## First-100-users plan (concrete, week-by-week)

| Week | Channel | Action | Target |

| 4 | Referral | Add "share this audit" prominent on results page; track shared-link conversions. | 25–40 of the next 100 from word of mouth |

## Unfair channel

**The shareable result URL itself.** When a founder runs the audit and sees a $480/mo finding, the OG card they'd post on X says "$480/mo of AI spend leak — RightSize audit." That headline is a tweet that performs without our doing anything. Every audit is a billboard, automatically.

The closest analogue is _Wrapped_ — Spotify's annual share cards. The reason that channel works is the reveal is genuine, not generated for sharing. Our same constraint: we won't manufacture a savings number to make a shareable card; the card is honest.

## Week-1 traction definition

Not vanity. The metric I'd brag about in week 1:

- **50+ audits run by people who are not me, my friends, or VCs of mine.**
- **15+ shared their audit URL externally** (email or social).
- **8+ leads (email submitted on the results page).**
- **3+ replied to the lead email saying "yes, send me the consultation invite."**

If I hit those, the funnel is real and the rest is volume. If I miss the share rate, the OG card needs work. If I miss the lead rate, the CTA copy is wrong. Each metric points to the next thing to fix.
